Biography

Lin Yizhe is a Chinese youth collaborative pianist. She is currently admitted to the Artist Diploma (Level 8) program in Opera Repetiteur at the Royal Birmingham Conservatoire with a substantial scholarship, and will enroll in September 2025. Previously, she graduated from the Royal College of Music (RCM) with a Master's degree in Collaborative Piano, where she studied under Simon Lepper, renowned vocal coach Roger Vignoles, and official BBC accompanist Elizabeth Burley.

She began learning the piano at age 3 and received vocal training from age 12. She was admitted to the Shenzhen Arts School majoring in Vocal and Opera, and later graduated with a bachelor's degree in Music Education from the Zhejiang Conservatory of Music, where she received multiple scholarships including the First-Class Scholarship for Professional Excellence. She also won first prize in the youth division of the "Ludao Cup" Piano Competition.

As an active performer, Lin Yizhe has participated in a variety of performances, including symphonic choruses, marching bands, musicals, solo piano, and collaborative piano. She has performed in the Zhejiang Conservatory of Music's "Early Summer · Listening to the Wind" Collaborative Piano Concert, and the RCM Keyboard Festival Concert. She has also served as a piano accompanist for competitions such as the RCM Brooks van der Pump English Song Competition.

She has participated and been highly praised in masterclasses led by internationally recognized vocal coaches such as Pauliina Tukiainen and Markus Hadulla. She was also appointed as an "Overseas Dandelion Envoy" by the Central Institute of Socialism, actively participating in the international dissemination of Chinese culture and the mutual learning of civilizations.
